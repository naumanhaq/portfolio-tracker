On Survival

Diversification is for those who don't know what they're doing. This portfolio holds things that have survived—companies that existed before the current crop of MBAs and will outlast them.

Luxury goods. The kind where scarcity is structural, not marketing. Where the product improves with age while competitors chase volume.

The barbell: equity positions measured in years, options in weeks. No middle ground. Equity side eliminates the fragile. Vol side profits from other people's mistakes pricing uncertainty.

Those who need quarterly earnings calls to validate their thesis are not investors. They are bureaucrats with Bloomberg terminals.
